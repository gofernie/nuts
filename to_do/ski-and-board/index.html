Ski and Board

Ski Fernie Alpine Resort · Deep powder, big terrain, classic BC winter.

Skiing and boarding in Fernie is the ultimate mountain fix. With deep powder, wide-open bowls, and five alpine faces, the resort delivers epic days from first chair to last run. Locals chase storms, visitors chase that grin-that-won’t-quit feeling. Whether you carve groomers or float through glades, there’s terrain for every level. Après means hot drinks, patios, and stories that grow with each telling. A powder day here isn’t just good - it’s legendary.

Legendary snow, five bowls & mountain town après

Skiing and snowboarding in Fernie isn’t just a winter activity — it’s the heartbeat of the season. Every year, storms roll in off the Pacific and bury the Lizard Range in deep, dry powder, turning Fernie Alpine Resort into a playground for skiers and riders. Located just 10 minutes from downtown Fernie, the resort boasts over 2,500 acres of terrain, five legendary bowls, and a well-earned reputation for “steep and deep” conditions.

The mountain is laid out so everyone finds their flow. Advanced riders lap Currie Bowl and Snake Ridge, chasing powder stashes that linger long after first chair. Intermediates carve smooth arcs down Cruiser and Blueberry. Beginners build confidence on the gentle Mighty Moose area, where the learning zone is welcoming and easy to access. The views from the top are stunning — craggy peaks, endless forest, and that unmistakable Elk Valley light.

On-mountain eats are part of the ritual. Warm up at Lost Boys Café with a hot chocolate or lunch overlooking the valley, then end the day at the Griz Bar, Fernie’s classic après-ski spot. It’s been around since the early days of the hill — boots still on, stories flowing as fast as the pints.

Gear is easy to dial in thanks to GearHub Sports, Edge of the World, and Commit Snow & Skate. For those looking for something extraordinary, Island Lake Lodge offers world-renowned cat skiing just minutes from town — a bucket-list powder day.

After the lifts stop spinning, downtown Fernie comes alive. Grab coffee at Rooftop Coffee Roasters or dinner at The Brickhouse or Nevados. It’s all part of the rhythm of winter here: big snow, great terrain, and a mountain town that’s easy to love.
